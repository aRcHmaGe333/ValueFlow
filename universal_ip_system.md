# ValueFlow: Universal IP Revenue Sharing System
*Transforming Intellectual Property from Scarcity to Abundance*

**PRIOR ART DISCLOSURE - AUGUST 29, 2025, 11:46 PM CEST (Stockholm)**

## Executive Summary

The current intellectual property system creates artificial scarcity through restrictive licensing, unused patent portfolios, and gatekeeping mechanisms that stifle innovation. This document proposes ValueFlow, a Universal IP Revenue Sharing System that enables automatic value sharing between original IP holders and IP reusers, designed to reduce or eliminate licensing barriers while ensuring fair compensation for all participants in the innovation ecosystem. This disclosure describes the core concepts, multiple non-limiting implementation approaches, and various allocation models that can be employed, including but not limited to equal splits, configurable ratios, multi-party distributions, and dynamic formulas.

## The Problem with Current IP Systems

### Patent Hoarding
Corporations accumulate thousands of patents that sit unused in storage, creating legal minefields that prevent innovation rather than encouraging it. Research shows that over 95% of patents are never commercialized by their original holders.

### Licensing Bottlenecks
Current licensing requires extensive legal negotiations, often taking months or years to secure rights. This creates barriers that favor large corporations with dedicated legal teams over individual innovators and small businesses.

### Innovation Suppression
The "all rights reserved" model incentivizes IP holders to block competitors rather than collaborate, leading to duplicated research efforts and slower technological progress.

## Core Innovation: ValueFlow Protocol

### Core Concepts and Definitions

Before describing the protocol, it is important to establish clear definitions:

- **Value**: Any monetary or economic benefit derived from the use, sale, licensing, or exploitation of intellectual property, including but not limited to direct sales revenue, subscription fees, licensing income, advertising revenue, or other forms of compensation.

- **Transaction**: Any exchange, transfer, or generation of value associated with intellectual property, whether occurring in real-time, batched periodically, or settled through deferred mechanisms.

- **Derivative Work**: Any creation, modification, adaptation, improvement, remix, or incorporation of existing intellectual property into a new work, product, or service.

- **Attribution/Provenance**: The record of original creators and the chain of derivative works, which may be tracked through various mechanisms including distributed ledgers, centralized registries, federated databases, or hybrid systems.

- **Allocation Rule**: The formula, ratio, or methodology used to distribute value among IP creators and users, which may be fixed, configurable, dynamic, time-varying, or based on complex multi-factor calculations.

- **Settlement**: The process by which allocated value is transferred to recipients, which may occur through various mechanisms including real-time payment processing, periodic batch transfers, escrow arrangements, clearinghouse systems, invoicing, or reporting-based distributions.

### Value Sharing Architecture

Any copyrighted or patented work can be made available under the ValueFlow system. Value generated from the use of intellectual property is automatically or semi-automatically shared according to configurable allocation rules. 

**Non-Limiting Example - Equal Split Model:**
In one illustrative embodiment, value may be shared equally:
- 50% to original IP creator
- 50% to derivative innovator

However, the system is not limited to this specific ratio and can accommodate numerous alternative allocation models, including but not limited to:
- **Configurable Ratios**: Any predetermined split (e.g., 70/30, 60/40, 80/20, 90/10)
- **Multi-Party Splits**: Distribution among three or more parties with arbitrary percentages
- **Dynamic Formulas**: Allocation based on factors such as usage volume, revenue thresholds, time since creation, or market conditions
- **Time-Varying Splits**: Ratios that change over time (e.g., declining original creator share, escalating innovator share)
- **Caps and Floors**: Minimum guarantees or maximum limits on any party's share
- **Lineage Graphs**: Complex attribution through multiple generations of derivative works, with value flowing through chains or trees of creators

This flexibility creates a symbiotic relationship where original creators benefit from adaptations, remixes, and evolutions of their work, while innovators gain access to intellectual property under transparent, predictable terms.

### Cooperative Innovation Ecosystem
ValueFlow transforms competition into collaboration. Instead of fighting over ownership, creators focus on building value that flows naturally to all participants. A musician benefits when their melody gets remixed, a engineer profits when their design gets improved, a writer earns from every adaptation of their story.

### Abundance Through Sharing
ValueFlow operates on the principle that ideas become more valuable when shared, not less. Every adaptation, improvement, and creative interpretation increases the original work's cultural and economic footprint. A single song might spawn thousands of remixes, covers, and variations - each one sending value back to the original creator while generating new value for the innovator.

### Low-Friction Collaboration

The ValueFlow system is designed to reduce or eliminate many barriers present in traditional licensing:
- Licensing negotiations may be reduced or eliminated depending on implementation
- Approval processes can be streamlined or removed
- Value distribution can be automated or semi-automated at point of exchange
- Tracking may be implemented through various network architectures (centralized, federated, or decentralized)
- Can be designed for compatibility across multiple creative and technical domains

The specific degree of friction reduction depends on the chosen implementation approach, technological infrastructure, and governance model.

### Viral Value Creation
Even unsuccessful derivative works increase the original IP's ecosystem value through exposure, market validation, and cultural resonance. Failed remixes still boost awareness. Abandoned adaptations still demonstrate market interest. Every attempt creates ripples of value that flow back to the source.

## ValueFlow in Practice: Real-World Scenarios

### Creative Industries

**Non-Limiting Example:**
A photographer's image gets used in 1,000 different projects - magazine covers, website headers, art installations, NFT collections. Instead of complex licensing deals, ValueFlow can automatically or semi-automatically send a predetermined portion of each project's value back to the photographer while enabling creative freedom for users. The specific percentage, timing, and mechanism of value transfer can vary based on the chosen implementation.

### Technology Development
An engineer's battery design gets improved by 50 different teams worldwide. Each improvement generates value for both the original inventor and the enhancing team. The original design becomes more valuable with each iteration, creating exponential returns for foundational innovations.

### Scientific Research
Academic research flows freely between institutions, with automatic value sharing ensuring researchers benefit from practical applications of their work. A chemistry paper's methodology gets used in 200 different products, generating sustainable funding for continued research.

### Cultural Evolution
Stories, music, and art evolve naturally through collective creativity. Traditional folklore becomes modern multimedia, with value flowing to cultural communities. Remixes and covers enrich both the original artists and the innovative interpreters.

## Technical Architecture

### Implementation Approaches and Non-Limiting Alternatives

The ValueFlow system can be implemented through numerous technological and organizational approaches. The following sections describe various non-limiting alternatives and options for each component of the system.

### Network Architecture Options

ValueFlow may operate through various network architectures, including but not limited to:

**Decentralized Networks:**
- Distributed ledger technology (blockchain, directed acyclic graphs, etc.)
- Peer-to-peer networks with no central authority
- Consensus-based validation and record-keeping

**Centralized Systems:**
- Traditional database architecture with a single authority
- Cloud-based platforms managed by a trusted entity
- Existing payment processor infrastructure

**Federated Models:**
- Multiple cooperating authorities or regional systems
- Cross-institutional networks with shared protocols
- Industry consortia managing collective registries

**Hybrid Approaches:**
- Combinations of centralized and decentralized elements
- Layered architectures with different trust models at each layer
- Bridge systems connecting multiple network types

### Settlement Mechanism Variants

Value distribution can be implemented through various settlement mechanisms:

**Real-Time Settlement:**
- Immediate payment processing at point of transaction
- Streaming micropayments
- Instant settlement through payment processors

**Batch Settlement:**
- Periodic aggregation and distribution (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Reduces transaction costs for high-volume, low-value transactions
- Allows for reconciliation and validation before transfer

**Escrow-Based Settlement:**
- Funds held by third party until conditions are met
- Release triggered by milestones, time periods, or mutual agreement
- Provides security and dispute resolution mechanisms

**Clearinghouse Models:**
- Central or federated clearing entities that net transactions
- Reduces overall payment volume through offsetting
- Common in financial services and inter-bank transfers

**Payment Processor Splits:**
- Integration with existing payment platforms (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
- Automatic division of incoming payments at processing layer
- Leverages existing financial infrastructure

**Invoice and Reporting-Based:**
- Periodic reporting of usage and value generation
- Invoice-based collection and distribution
- Suitable for B2B scenarios and enterprise implementations

**Hybrid Settlement:**
- Different mechanisms for different transaction types or value thresholds
- Tiered approaches (real-time for large amounts, batch for small)
- Context-dependent settlement based on industry, jurisdiction, or agreement

### Smart Contract and Automation Options

Automation of revenue sharing may be implemented through various approaches:

**Blockchain Smart Contracts:**
In one embodiment, blockchain-based smart contracts can automatically execute revenue splits in real-time, with every transaction triggering immediate payment distribution without intermediaries or delays.

**Traditional Automated Systems:**
- Database triggers and scheduled jobs in conventional IT infrastructure
- Integration with existing ERP and financial systems
- Automated but not necessarily using distributed ledger technology

**Manual with Digital Assistance:**
- Software tools that calculate and recommend distributions
- Human approval required before execution
- Suitable for high-value or complex transactions requiring review

**API-Based Integration:**
- Programmatic interfaces for third-party integration
- Event-driven architectures with webhooks and callbacks
- Microservices-oriented implementations

### IP Registry Implementations

The system may include a registry of participating intellectual property, which can be implemented through various approaches:

**Comprehensive Universal Registry:**
A centralized or distributed database of all participating intellectual property with standardized metadata:
- Original creation date and creator(s)
- Usage history and derivative works
- Revenue generation metrics
- Quality and success ratings from market performance

**Federated Registry Networks:**
- Multiple interconnected registries maintained by different entities
- Industry-specific or jurisdiction-specific registries
- Cross-registry lookup and synchronization protocols

**Optional or Minimal Registry:**
- Lightweight systems that register only essential claims
- On-demand registration at first use
- Cryptographic claims without centralized storage

**Hybrid Registry Models:**
- Combination of public and private registry components
- Tiered registration with different detail levels
- Integration with existing IP offices and copyright registries

## Economic Benefits

### Edge Cases and Special Scenarios

Real-world implementation requires handling various complex situations:

**Refunds and Chargebacks:**
- When a transaction is refunded, the system may claw back distributed shares, adjust future distributions, or absorb the loss through reserves or insurance mechanisms
- Chargeback handling may involve dispute resolution to determine whether the original distribution should be reversed
- Different policies may apply based on the reason for refund (buyer's remorse vs. fraud vs. defective product)

**Taxes and Withholding:**
- The system may integrate with tax reporting requirements in various jurisdictions
- Withholding for taxes, social security, or other mandatory deductions can occur at distribution time
- Different tax treatment for different types of IP or revenue streams
- Cross-border tax considerations and treaty compliance

**Dispute Holds:**
- When attribution or allocation is disputed, funds may be held in escrow
- Graduated release as disputes are partially resolved
- Insurance or bonding to cover potential disputes
- Time limits for dispute filing and resolution

**Fraud Detection and Recovery:**
- Mechanisms to identify and respond to fraudulent claims or transactions
- Recovery processes for improperly distributed funds
- Penalties or exclusion for bad actors
- Victim compensation schemes

**Bankruptcy and Legal Claims:**
- Treatment of revenue-sharing obligations in bankruptcy proceedings
- Priority of revenue-sharing claims relative to other creditors
- Continuation or termination of sharing upon insolvency
- Assignment or transfer of revenue-sharing rights

**Death, Incapacity, and Succession:**
- Transfer of revenue-sharing rights to heirs or estates
- Corporate succession and merger scenarios
- Trust and estate planning integration
- Duration of post-mortem revenue sharing (may align with copyright terms or be different)

**Complex Multi-Generational Derivatives:**
- Handling chains of derivative works (A creates original, B creates derivative, C creates derivative of B's work)
- Various models: cascading percentages, total pool division, upstream-only attribution
- Circular dependencies and how to resolve them
- Opt-out or buyout provisions for simplifying complex chains
